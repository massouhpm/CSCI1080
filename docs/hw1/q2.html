Guitars

Types of Guitars

Acoustic Guitars

These instruments produce sound solely through the vibration of the strings.
What allows them to do this so well is their hollow body design.

Electric Guitars

These instruments contain built in pickups that convert the string vibrations into an electric signals that are then played through a guitar amplifier.
Although they still are able to produce sound in the same manner as an acoustic, they are generally built with solid bodies so that there is less resonation.

Wikipedia is a free online encyclopedia, created and edited by volunteers around the world and hosted by the Wikimedia Foundation.

"The guitar is a musical instrument classified as a string instrument with anywhere from four to 18 strings, usually having six. The sound is projected either acoustically, using a hollow wooden or plastic and wood box (for an acoustic guitar), or through electrical amplifier and a speaker (for an electric guitar). It is typically played by strumming or plucking the strings with the fingers, thumb and/or fingernails of the right hand or with a pick while fretting (or pressing against the frets) the strings with the fingers of the left hand."
